\centerline{\bf A derivation to the vanished couple}
\vskip .5cm
As we have assumed, the force applied to the surface is
$$
\underline{F}_{n}
= \int \! \! \! \int_{D} {\rm d} \underline{F}_{n}
= \int \! \! \! \int_{D} \underline{\tau}_{n} \, {\rm d} A_{n}.
$$
Similarly, the couple applied to it, with respect to some point~$P$ is
$$
\underline{M}_{n, P}
= \int \! \! \! \int_{D} \underline{r}_{n, P} \times {\rm d} \underline{F}_{n}, \eqno(1)
$$
and if the `stress~couple' applied to~${\rm d} A_{n}$ is denoted as~$\underline{\omega}_{n}$,
the couple can be also written as
$$
\underline{M}_{n, P}
= \int \! \! \! \int_{D} \underline{\omega}_{n} \, {\rm d} A_{n}
+ \int \! \! \! \int_{D} \underline{r}_{n, P} \times {\rm d} \underline{F}_{n}. \eqno(2)
$$
Conparing with Eq. (1) and~(2), and they give
$$
\int \! \! \! \int_{D} \underline{\omega}_{n} \, {\rm d} A_{n} = \underline{0}.
$$
Since the closed region~$D$ can be chosen arbitarily, and~$\underline{\omega}_{n}$ is supposed to be continuous over~$D$,
$\underline{\omega}_{n}$ has to be~$\underline{0}$ everywhere.

Or, we can use some bruteforce to prove that:
$$
\eqalign{\underline{\omega}_{n}
&= \lim_{A_{n} \to 0} {\underline{M}_{n} \over A_{n}}
 = \lim_{A_{n} \to 0} {\int \! \! \! \int_{D} \underline{r}_{n} \times {\rm d} \underline{F}_{n} \over A_{n}}
 = \lim_{A_{n} \to 0} {\int \! \! \! \int_{D} \underline{r}_{n} \times \underline{\tau}_{n} \, {\rm d} A_{n} \over A_{n}}, \cr
 \hbox{so} \quad \Vert \underline{\omega}_{n} \Vert
&\le \lim_{A_{n} \to 0} {1 \over A_{n}} {\int \! \! \! \int_{D} \Vert \underline{r}_{n} \Vert \cdot \Vert \underline{\tau}_{n} \Vert \, {\rm d} A_{n}} \cr
&\le \lim_{A_{n} \to 0} \Vert \underline{r}_{n} \Vert_{\max} \Vert \underline{\tau}_{n} \Vert_{\max} = \underline{0},
 \quad \hbox{which is equivalent to} \quad \underline{\omega}_{n} = \underline{0},}
$$
as long as we restrict the shape of~$D$ so that~$\Vert \underline{r}_{n} \Vert_{\max}$ converges to~$0$ as~$A_{n}$ does.
\vfill\eject
